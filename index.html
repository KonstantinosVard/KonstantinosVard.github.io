University of Ioannina 2022-2024 GPA: 9.80/10

Thesis: Synthesis and DFT Study of Thioether-Xanthone Photoinitiators for MMA Polymerization

Research | Supervised by Prof. Siskos Michael

This work combined synthesis, photophysical/photochemical characterization, and DFT (M06-2X/B3LYP) calculations to decode the mechanisms of novel photoinitiators. Key findings:

  • Computational Modeling: M06-2X outperformed B3LYP for these large molecules due to superior dispersion force treatment.
  • Triplet-State Dynamics: DFT revealed multiple triplet conformations; Boltzmann distributions dictated kinetics, enabling more controlled reactivity.
  • Polymerization Mechanisms: Efficiency depended on both bond cleavage energy and monomer-mediated triplet quenching.
  • Computational Analysis: Custom Python tools were crucial for extracting kinetic constants from complex spectra and optimizing theoretical models.

Other Academic Highlights:

  • Thesis awarded a perfect 10.0/10.0 grade.
